Expressing a LP in Canonical Form

We use Canonical form to express a linear programming problem(LP) in preparation for our Simplex Algorithm. For the following LP:

        min \(-x_1 + 2x_2 + 2x_3 + x_4 \)

such that:

        \(x_1 + x_2 - x_3 = 5 \)
        \(x_1 + x_2 - x_4 \le 10 \)
        \(x_2 - x_3 + x_4 \ge 9 \)
        \(3 \le x_1 \le 4 \)
        \(x_2, x_3, x_4 \ge 0 \)

We have it's Canonical Form being:

        max \(x_1 - 2x_2 - 2x_3 - x_4 \)

such that:

        \(x_1 + x_2 - x_3 + a_1 = 5 \)
        \(x_1 + x_2 - x_4 + s_2 = 10 \)
        \(x_2 - x_3 + x_4 + s_3 + a_3 = 9 \)
        \(x_1 - s_4 + a_4 = 3 \)
        \(x_1 + s_5 = 4 \)
        \(x_1, x_2, x_3, x_4 \ge 0 \)
        \(s_2, s_3, s_4, s_5 \ge 0 \)
        \(a_1, a_3, a_4 \ge 0 \)

Here where we have a \(=\) we just add an artificial variable \(a_i\),
where we have \(\le\), we add a slack variable \(s_i\),
where we have \(\ge\), we add an artificial variable \(a_i\) and subtract a slack variable \(s_i\).

Note, sometimes you will have to be creative with constraints and manipulate them to fit the form, for example: \(3 \ge x_1 \le 4 \), which is broken into two constraints \(3 \le x_1 \) and \(x_1 \le 4 \).
